Amelia Earhart

Amelia Mary Earhart was an American aviation pioneer and author. Earhart was the first female aviator to fly solo across the Atlantic Ocean. She received the U.S. Distinguished Flying Cross for this accomplishment. In 1935, Earhart became a visiting faculty member at Purdue University as an advisor to aeronautical engineering and a career counselor to women students.

  • 1897- Born July 24th, 1987
  • 1909 - Started Public School after being Home Schooled
  • 1927 - Flew the first official flight out of Dennison Airport
  • 1928 - Becomes first woman to cross Atlantic as passenger with Pilot Charles Lindbergh
  • 1932 - Completes Transatlantic Flight as Pilot, becoming the first female pilot to make the journey.
  • 1937 - Sets off on World Flight accompanied by crew.Stopping with her crew to fix issues with the plane, Amelia experiences issues while trying to take off again, resulting in a severely damaged plane. It was decided the flight was to be postponed. Makes a second attempt, this time unpublicized and changing routes. It was during this flight that Earhart and crew member Fred Noonan disappear.
